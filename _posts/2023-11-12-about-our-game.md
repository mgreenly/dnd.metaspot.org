# 2023-11-12 About Our Game

I'm sure that I'll provide more in depth posts in the future about all the stuff I discuss below but here's a quick overview of our game.  My primary goal has been to create a fun, very running campaign and by long I mean "very long", a decade or more. A campaign where generations of characters go from zero to max level. A campaign that involves a large number of players all helping to build a giant shared world through actual play.  All subsequent decisions have been made to support that goal.

## Sandbox

Our game is a sandbox game. That means there are no long running story arcs.  Instead there are a number of NPCs that have goals they work towards over time, it's assumed that many of these goals will conflict with the characters plans.  Periodically as we play those NPCs take actions to advance towards their goals, creating situations that the players can choose to get involved with, or not, it's always their choice. I've chosen this style of game based on previous experiences trying to run long running campaigns with story arcs. I personally found that at some point that approach turns into work and becomes quite un-fun for the DM. After 65+ sessions I'm convinced the sandbox was the correct choice.

## Rules

I've elected to use [Old School Essentials: Advanced Fantasy rules](https://preview.drivethrurpg.com/en/product/336584/old-school-essentials-advanced-fantasy-player-s-tome).  I made this choice after a lot of research prior to starting the game.  I became convinced that classic TSR era D&D lends itself to long running sandbox campaigns better than modern rules and I found OSE:AF to be the best, readily available, retro clone at the time.  I'm extremely happy with that choice and intend to continue using OSE:AF as written but will be expanding on those rules with additions from the [Dungeons & Dragons Rules Cyclopedia](https://preview.drivethrurpg.com/en/product/17171/DD-Rules-Cyclopedia-Basic).  Specifically weapon mastery and non-weapon proficiencies.

## Schedule

We play weekly, on Saturday nights, with a relatively large group.  My goals is to maintain 7 regular players.  This helps to insulate the game from absences.  It is assumed that over time players will come and go. I may get lucky and hold on to some players for 10 years but it's not realistic that will be typical.
